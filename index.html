Supplementary Words from Harriet Cade, Head Editor

"Aura Aura is a photobook without text, but it is not silent. In this definitive third edition, the Cooperative presents our collection of images capturing the inhabited architectures of Ganymede’s great domes: The monumental city-habitats that hold a living threshold between ice and sky.

Across its pages, light streams through the geodesic panes of reinforced glass, pooling against immense planes of astcrete and the deep, honeyed warmth of wood imported from Earth, decades ago, shaped into railings, panels, benches, sills. While the G.A.P. continues to raise questions about our shared future, these photographs depict neither ruins nor utopias, rather, voidborn continuities of human form and intention.

The camera lingers on the quiet conjunctions of the organic and the engineered: synchronized drilling rigs in a slow ballet (the industry of survival); a lone dog resting in the vastness of a square before the working day begins; lichen taking hold on the painted arches of a service terminal roof, its pattern an unapologetic rebirth of earthly weatherings. In these so-called juxtapositions, the viewer might encounter a certain meditation on survival and meaning: Architecture as the vessel of a dispersed, enduring humanity.

At times, beyond the domes, Jupiter rises, immense and silent, or the auroras shimmer, refracting through the glass like our very own cosmic curtains. Perhaps, if you'll permit a self-indulgence, Aura Aura invites contemplation not only of structure and design, but of belonging, of how space becomes place, and how even out here, the shapes of care and craft persist.

The book remains intentionally wordless, yet each photograph carries myriad voices: the steamy hum of atmospheric machinery, the glossy tip-tap of corridors, the brush of regolite pavers, all the shared inheritance of form. It is our chronicle of flourishing through endurance, of history braided with experiment, of the enduring effort to build warmth against the cold.

The 3rd and final edition of Aura Aura is dedicated to my brother, Elias, whose disappearance is a window left open on a long, frigid night. You are loved, Elias, a warmth I hope you can feel even in the vacuum of your absence."
